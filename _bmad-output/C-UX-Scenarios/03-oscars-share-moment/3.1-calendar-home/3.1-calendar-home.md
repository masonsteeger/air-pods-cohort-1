## Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Let Oscar verify his group is listed accurately, confirm the page is credible enough to represent his community, and copy the stable URL for sharing in his own channels.

**Entry Context:**
Inspection visit from an Improving Slack announcement. Oscar is not a discoverer — he already knows what the calendar is; he's checking whether it's good enough to share.

**Exit Action:**
Copies the URL from the browser address bar. Closes the tab. Scenario completes on this page.

## First-Step Entry Context

### User Situation (from Q3)

Volunteer who runs a DFW meetup at Improving's space. Passionate but stretched thin — growth constraint is reach, not content. Saturday afternoon, composing his monthly newsletter to his mailing list. An Improving Slack announcement mentioned the public calendar is live; he's considering including it in the newsletter as an "here's the rest of the community" anchor.

### Mental State (from Q4)

- **Hope:** Confirm his group is listed accurately and the page looks professional enough to represent his community.
- **Worry:** His group is missing or misnamed, or the page looks amateur, and sharing it would reflect poorly on him.

### Discovery Method (from Q6)

Clicked the calendar link from an Improving announcement in his Slack. Landed on Calendar Home. Inspection visit, not discovery.

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

Interactions that **stay on this page** for Oscar's scenario:

1. **Find-my-group state**
   Oscar scrolls/scans the list searching for his group's name. Group names must be prominent on every row — in MVP there is no filter-by-group, so scroll-scan is the primary mechanism.

2. **Verify-listing state**
   Oscar taps/hovers a row to see the full event detail and verifies it matches his own Meetup page (same date, same title, same location). Uses the same row-expand interaction canonically designed in `1.1-calendar-home`.

3. **Trust-the-brand state**
   Oscar's eyes move to the page header and URL. Is it clearly "Improving Community Calendar"? Does the URL look stable (no `?session=abc123`)? Decision point: share or don't share.

4. **Copy-URL state**
   Oscar copies the URL from the browser address bar. No in-page "share this" affordance is strictly needed for MVP (the address bar is the share mechanism) — but an optional "copy link" button could reinforce the behavior if we want to invest later.

## Relationship to 1.1-calendar-home

This spec is a **scenario-3 lens** on the same underlying page canonically designed in [1.1-calendar-home](../../01-petes-unified-discovery/1.1-calendar-home/1.1-calendar-home.md).

Oscar's scenario adds two design requirements that Pete's and Imani's didn't emphasize:

- **Identity anchor** — the page must carry a visible "Improving Community Calendar" identity (header/branding). Pete arrives via LinkedIn and already knows what it is from the referrer post. Imani arrives via bookmark and has institutional context. Oscar arrives needing to verify *his recipients* will know what they're landing on — so the page itself must carry that context.
- **URL cleanliness** — Oscar's share mechanism is the browser address bar. Pete and Imani don't copy URLs; Oscar does. The URL must be the canonical stable form always.
