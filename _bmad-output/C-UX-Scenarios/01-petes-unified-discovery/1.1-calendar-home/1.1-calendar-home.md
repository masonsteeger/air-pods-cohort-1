## Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Let Pete scan all upcoming Improving-hosted events at a glance, identify one that's worth his time, see enough inline detail to feel confident, and hand off to Meetup to RSVP.

**Entry Context:**
First visit. Pete just tapped a link from a LinkedIn post by an Improver he follows, on his phone, at lunch. No prior context with the calendar brand — page must earn trust quickly.

**Exit Action:**
Tap **View on Meetup** on an event row → opens Meetup event page in a new tab (step 1.2, external).

---

## First-Step Entry Context

### User Situation (from Q3)

Mid-career DFW developer (~38), actively looking to plug into professional community. He's been hearing about Improving-hosted groups for years but keeps giving up on tracking them — every group lives on its own Meetup page, and checking them all feels like a chore. Weekday lunch break.

### Mental State (from Q4)

- **Hope:** Find 2–3 relevant DFW tech events in the next month at a glance, without clicking through twenty Meetup pages.
- **Worry:** This turns out to be another half-baked or stale calendar that makes him question whether the events are actually happening.

### Discovery Method (from Q6)

Scrolling LinkedIn at lunch, tapped a post from an Improver he follows linking to "all Improving-hosted tech meetups in one place." No prior context with the calendar.

---

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

Interactions that **stay on this page** — surfaced as distinct states the design must support:

1. **Arrival / scan state**
   Pete sees the chronological list with nearest-date events at the top. Must be scannable on mobile without scrolling past chrome.

2. **Row-expanded state**
   Pete taps an event row; inline detail reveals — group name, date/time, location, short description if available in sheet, and a clear **View on Meetup** CTA. Stays on page; no navigation.

3. **Last-updated / freshness state**
   A page-level timestamp ("Last synced from source: 2 hours ago" or equivalent) is visible without scrolling. Addresses Pete's staleness worry and Dawn's data-accuracy blame concern simultaneously.

4. **Cancelled-event state**
   When a row represents a cancelled event, the row carries a visible cancellation marker and suppresses or visually de-emphasizes the "View on Meetup" CTA. Protects Pete and Oscar from wasted-trip fear.

5. **Empty / sync-failed state** *(edge case)*
   If the source sync fails or no upcoming events exist, a neutral "No upcoming events right now — try again soon" message appears with the freshness timestamp still visible. Avoids the impression that the calendar is broken; deflects blame from Dawn.

## Page-Level Design Requirements Surfaced by Other Scenarios

This page is also walked by scenarios 02 (Imani) and 03 (Oscar). They don't own its documentation, but they add requirements:

- **From Scenario 02 (Imani's Weekly Check-in):** The *nearest upcoming events* must be visible without deep scrolling so Imani can answer "what's in my building this week?" in one glance. The freshness indicator (item 3 above) also serves Imani's trust need.
- **From Scenario 03 (Oscar's Share Moment):** The page must carry a visible "Improving community calendar" identity anchor — group name/branding credible enough that Oscar is comfortable pasting the URL into his own community channels. The URL itself must look stable and professional.

Cross-scenario design decisions for this page are negotiated in Phase 4.
