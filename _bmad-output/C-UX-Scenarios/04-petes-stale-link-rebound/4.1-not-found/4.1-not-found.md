# 4.1 — Not Found (404 Page)

## Page Metadata

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Scenario** | 04: Pete's Stale Link Rebound |
| **Page Number** | 4.1 |
| **Platform** | Mobile (iPhone, mobile browser) — highest-risk device; desktop inherits |

---

## Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Reassure a visitor who hit a broken URL that they're in the right neighborhood, and give them one obvious way to get to the real destination.

**Entry Context:**
Visitor expected Calendar Home. Got a 404 because the URL was mistyped, mangled by a sharing app, or pointed at a path that has since changed. This is a moment of trust fragility — the entire visit depends on whether the 404 page feels intentional or accidental.

**Exit Action:**
Tap "Go to the calendar" CTA → navigate to Calendar Home (step 4.2 / scenario 01's territory).

---

## First-Step Entry Context

### User Situation (from Q3)

Mid-career DFW developer (~38), actively looking to plug into professional community. He tapped a shared link from LinkedIn (or a Google result, or someone else's email) expecting the Improving Community Calendar. The URL failed. He's on his phone, mid-scroll, attention fragile.

### Mental State (from Q4)

- **Hope:** Get to the thing he was promised despite a bad URL.
- **Worry:** The whole calendar is vaporware; this was a waste of a tap; he closes the tab and doesn't try again.

### Discovery Method (from Q6)

Tapped a shared link from LinkedIn on mobile. URL mangled or stale. Expected Calendar Home; got this 404 page.

---

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

Interactions that **stay on this page**:

1. **Arrival / reassurance state**
   The page loads. Copy is friendly and self-explanatory — ideally a single sentence. No red, no stack trace, no "ERROR." Warm tone matching the brand voice ("welcoming without marketing fluff" per brief). Suggested copy: *"This page doesn't exist — but the Improving Community Calendar does."*

2. **Identity anchor state**
   "Improving Community Calendar" brand (header or clearly-visible text) is present on the 404 itself. Even though this specific URL failed, Pete needs to know he's in the right neighborhood — the calendar isn't vaporware, he just took a wrong turn.

3. **Single CTA state**
   One button / link. "Go to the calendar." Thumb-reachable on mobile. No site nav, no search box, no "did you mean…" — just the one obvious next action.

---

## Design Constraints

- **No dead ends.** The 404 must always offer a way forward. No empty content states allowed.
- **Mobile-first.** Most 404s on this site will originate from mangled-mobile-share paths. Thumb-reachable CTA is mandatory; hamburger menus are not.
- **Brand-consistent.** The 404 is not a throwaway system page; it's a micro-experience that determines whether hard-won referral traffic converts or evaporates.
