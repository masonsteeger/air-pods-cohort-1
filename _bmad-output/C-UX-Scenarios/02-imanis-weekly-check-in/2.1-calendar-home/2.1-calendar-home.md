## Overview

**Page Purpose:**
Let Imani scan all upcoming events to quickly identify ones in her own office this week. Success is a mental bookmark of a date and time — no click-through required.

**Entry Context:**
Returning bookmark-style visit. Imani typed the first few letters of the URL; browser autocompleted from history. No external channel involved.

**Exit Action:**
Closes the tab. Scenario completes on this page — no outbound click. Success is satisfied-and-gone, not a conversion moment.

---

## First-Step Entry Context

### User Situation (from Q3)

Senior Improving consultant who plans her week intentionally and is protective of her time. She's walked past buzzing meetup rooms in her own building for years without knowing what was inside. A coworker shared the calendar link a few months back; she bookmarked it. Monday morning, 8:50am at the Plano office, coffee in hand, laptop booting.

### Mental State (from Q4)

- **Hope:** Spot in seconds any event in her own Plano office this week that's worth 30 minutes of her time.
- **Worry:** The list is chronological across all sites, she can't tell at a glance which events are *hers*, misses her building's events anyway, and goes back to walking past closed doors.

### Discovery Method (from Q6)

Opens a new tab, types the first few letters of the calendar URL — browser autocompletes from history. Roughly 3 seconds from intent to landing. A returning-visitor pattern, not a discovery moment.

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

Interactions that **stay on this page** for Imani's scenario:

1. **Scan-for-location state**
   Imani's eyes filter the chronological list on the location column looking for "Plano" (or whichever office is hers). Mobile and desktop both must surface location prominently enough for this scan to work in seconds.

2. **Row-expanded state** *(shared with scenario 01)*
   Tapping a row reveals topic and time detail inline. Imani uses this to verify the event is worth her time before mentally reserving it. Same mechanism as Pete's scenario; documented canonically in `1.1-calendar-home`.

3. **This-week affordance** *(design requirement, not a state)*
   Nearest-date events must appear at the top of the page above the fold on desktop. Imani's weekly check should be a 10-second operation, not a scroll hunt. Chronological ordering (already planned for scenario 01) satisfies this if — and only if — the first screenful reliably contains the week's events.

4. **Freshness signal** *(shared with scenario 01)*
   Same "Last synced from source" timestamp, serving Imani's Monday-morning freshness need. No new design work.
