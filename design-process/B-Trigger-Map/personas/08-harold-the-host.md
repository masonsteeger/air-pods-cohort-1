# Persona: Harold the Host

> Priority 2b — Internal Employee (Event Host)

**Created:** 2026-03-05
**Project:** air-pods-cohort-1

---

## Who Harold Is

Harold is a mid-level developer at Improving who volunteered to be an event host for user group nights. He's the face of Improving when external attendees walk through the door — he greets them, makes sure they're comfortable, points them to food, and represents the company in a warm, approachable way. He coordinates directly with organizers like Oscar on event night and works with Dawn on logistics beforehand. He's been at Improving for three years and genuinely loves the people side of tech.

---

## Psychological Profile

Harold is a **natural connector** who volunteered for hosting because it aligns with who he already is — the person at the conference who knows everyone, the colleague who introduces people who should meet. Nobody assigned him this role; he sought it out because Improving's community values match his own.

Harold sees hosting as more than setting up chairs. He's a **soft brand ambassador** who takes pride in making visitors feel that Improving is a special place. When Pete walks in for the first time, Harold wants him to leave thinking "those Improving people are great." He loves handing out company swag — it's a small gesture that makes visitors feel welcomed and gives him a natural conversation starter.

Underneath the warmth, Harold has a **strategic instinct**. He listens for signals: a visitor mentions they're job hunting, a startup founder complains about scaling challenges, a developer asks about consulting. Harold doesn't hard-sell — he connects. "You should talk to our data team" or "We actually help companies with that." He sees every event as a chance to **expand Improving's network organically**.

Harold is distinct from Imani in a key way: Imani is a **consumer** of the community (she wants to attend and learn), while Harold is a **facilitator** of the community (he wants to make events successful and connect people). Imani checks the calendar to plan her week; Harold checks the calendar to prepare for his hosting duties.

---

## Internal State

When Harold thinks about hosting, he feels **energized and purposeful**. It's the part of his week that doesn't feel like work. He takes **personal pride** when an event goes smoothly — good turnout, visitors engaged, organizer happy. When attendance is low or a visitor seems lost, he feels **responsible**, like he could have done more to help.

He has a quiet **competitive streak** about Improving's reputation. He wants every visitor's experience to be better than whatever they had at the last venue. The swag, the food, the welcome — it all matters to him.

---

## Usage Context

Harold checks the calendar early in the week to see **what's coming up and who's hosting what**. He needs to know: Which group is meeting? How many typically attend? Who's the organizer? Is there anything special this week (guest speaker, demo night)? This lets him prepare — order enough food, set up the right room configuration, and mentally gear up for the crowd.

On event night, the calendar is already behind him — he's in the room. But beforehand, he uses it as his **preparation tool**. He might also share the calendar link with visitors: "Check this out — we host 20 groups here. Come back anytime."

**Access/Discovery:** Checks weekly as part of hosting prep, shares link with visitors
**Emotional State:** Energized (preparing), proud (during events), responsible (when things go wrong)
**Behavior Pattern:** Monday/Tuesday planning check, event-night execution, post-event visitor follow-up
**Decision Criteria:** "What's happening this week? How many people? What do I need to prepare?"
**Success Outcome:** Visitor says "This is a great space — I'll come back" or asks about Improving's services

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Maximize Community Visibility:** Harold is the human embodiment of visibility — every visitor he greets learns about Improving's community investment firsthand
- ✅ **Increase Event Attendance:** Harold's warm welcome and follow-up encourage repeat attendance; he's the reason one-time visitors become regulars
- ✅ **Reduce Operational Friction:** Harold coordinates with Dawn on logistics; the calendar gives him self-serve information so he doesn't need to ask Dawn "what's this week?"
