# Persona: Prospective Pete (Pete the Prospect)

> Priority 1 — External Community Member

**Created:** 2026-03-05
**Project:** air-pods-cohort-1

---

## Who Pete Is

Pete is a mid-career software developer in the DFW area who attends 1-2 meetups a month when he knows about them. He moved to Dallas 18 months ago from Austin and lost his established meetup network. He's heard Improving hosts groups but has no idea which ones, when, or how to find them.

---

## Psychological Profile

Pete values **continuous learning and professional community** — he knows his career grows faster when he's plugged into local tech circles. He's not passive; he actively searches for groups, but his discovery tools are fragmented. He checks Meetup.com by topic, scans LinkedIn posts, and asks colleagues. The friction isn't motivation — it's **visibility**. He'd attend more events if he could find them.

Pete is **pragmatic about his time**. He won't spend 20 minutes hunting across five Meetup pages to piece together a schedule. He wants a single view: what's happening, when, where, is it relevant to me? If that view doesn't exist, he defaults to the 2-3 groups he already knows and misses everything else.

He has a mild **fear of showing up to the wrong thing** — walking into a room where the topic is way outside his expertise or the group culture doesn't fit. Seeing group descriptions, average attendance, and topic areas in advance reduces this anxiety.

---

## Internal State

When Pete thinks about finding new meetups, he feels **mildly frustrated and resigned**. He knows there's more out there but doesn't have a good way to find it. When he discovers a new relevant group, he feels a spark of **excitement and connection** — "this is exactly what I was looking for."

---

## Usage Context

Pete finds the calendar via Google search ("tech meetups Dallas"), a colleague's link, or Improving's website. He's scanning for **relevance and logistics**: What topics? What night? Where exactly? He scans quickly — if the page loads slowly or looks outdated, he bounces. If he sees a well-organized list with dates, topics, and Meetup links, he opens 2-3 groups in new tabs and RSVPs within minutes. The calendar's job is to get him from "browsing" to "RSVP" in under 60 seconds.

**Access/Discovery:** Google search, colleague referral, Improving.com link
**Emotional State:** Mildly frustrated (searching), excited (discovering)
**Behavior Pattern:** Scans quickly, opens multiple tabs, decides fast
**Decision Criteria:** Topic relevance, date/time fit, group size/vibe
**Success Outcome:** RSVPs to 2-3 groups within 5 minutes of landing

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Maximize Community Visibility:** Pete IS the visibility goal — every new Pete who discovers the calendar validates Improving's investment
- ✅ **Increase Event Attendance:** Pete converts directly to attendance; removing his discovery barrier is the highest-leverage action
- ✅ **Reduce Operational Friction:** Pete currently emails Dawn or asks colleagues; the calendar eliminates these inquiries
